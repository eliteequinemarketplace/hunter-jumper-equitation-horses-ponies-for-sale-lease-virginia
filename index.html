Virginia is a premier destination for hunter, jumper, and equitation riders — known for its rich equestrian tradition and highly competitive show circuits throughout the state. With world-class competition hubs in regions like Middleburg, The Plains, Upperville, and Lexington, Virginia hosts a wide range of hunter and jumper events that attract riders from across the East Coast.

Upperville Colt & Horse Show

The Upperville Colt and Horse Show, held annually in June in Upperville, Virginia, is the oldest horse show in the United States — founded in 1853. Upperville hosts prestigious hunter, jumper, and equitation classes including the highly competitive Green Hunter Incentive classes and important hunter and jumper championships. Horses that have competed at Upperville carry the prestige of one of the most historic venues in American equestrian sport.

Middleburg & Virginia Horse Country

The Middleburg and Plains area of Northern Virginia is one of the most historically significant horse communities in the United States — a region defined by rolling countryside, centuries-old farming estates, and a deep equestrian culture. Many of the East Coast's top hunter jumper trainers and programs are based in Virginia's horse country, and buyers searching for quality hunter jumper horses for sale in Virginia will find an active and knowledgeable seller community.

HITS Culpeper & The Virginia Circuit

HITS Culpeper brings major hunter jumper competition to Virginia's horse country, providing Virginia-based horses with consistent show experience and competition records. The Virginia circuit also includes numerous recognized shows throughout the state, giving horses and riders year-round competitive opportunities close to the nation's capital and the broader Mid-Atlantic equestrian community.
